Microsoft Hardware

Microsoft has been constantly making hardware ever since the company was first created. As things continue to develop, when it comes to the gaming industry, here's the current list of hardware.

Controllers

There has been tons of new controllers microsoft makes throughout the years, currently, the xbox one controller is used for their latest games, despite the xbox one not being the latest console, the newer consoles use them since they had a great design. With bluetooth you can even use them on pc, thankfully, Valve's steam software supports xbox one controllers officially and lots of games including microsoft's games already have controller support. To purchase one, click the controller.

Consoles

Like most AAA companies, microsoft has always released new generations of the xbox consoles. Back in the early 2000's the first xbox was released, but they needed a game to be the face of the console, so they got bungie, a indie developer group to make their game the face of the console since microsoft loved the game so much. This game is called: "Halo: Combat Evolved". While plenty of other games existed on the console, the most memorable would be halo. Later, they released the Xbox 230 which would have better quality and function. The xbox 360 controllers were wireless unlike the original xbox and were powered by two AA baatteries. Followed by the Xbox one which was a powerful console using the xbox one controller. Of course while the Xbox one was great, they later made the Xbox One S which was more powerful, followed by the Xbox One Series S, more powerful than that. And recently, the new Xbox Series X, the most powerful console in the xbox family to date, by FAR. It even had Nvidia (a video graphics card company) to implement "Ray tracing" in a lot of it's games. Ray tracing is a setting that DRASTICALLY improves the visual quality of many games out there, mainly a lighting thing, the biggest example of good ray tracing would be Minecraft. So get the latest consoles, click the xbox console.
